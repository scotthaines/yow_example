Attributions

Attribution acknowledges the work of another.[1] Good attribution has several parts. These are

  • Indicate what is from another source.
  • title (Provide the title or description of what is from another source.)
  • author (Who created it.)
  • source (Where can you get it.)
  • license (Under what terms is this work provided.)
  • changes (If the work included is modified from the original, a derivative work, then describe the changes.)

YOW Example Attributions are provided for several reasons. These include:

  1. Some items included in YOW Example require attribution. These items from other sources have licenses or terms of use which require their attribution if they are included in another work. The CC BY 4.0 License Deed is an example. The Deed's attribution[ai] is required since the deed is explicitly included in YOW Example.[2]
  2. It is the right thing to do. "It is widely regarded as a sign of decency and respect to acknowledge the creator by giving him/her credit for the work."[3]
  3. Attributions in YOW Example serve as an example. If you write something from scratch then fill in a section like '"Your work's title" Attributions'. If you write something and include pages from YOW Example then both fill in a section like '"Your work's title" Attributions' where you will attribute YOW Example and other works as necessary, and include the parts of 'YOW Example Attributions' for what you included from YOW Example which in turn is from other sources.
  4. "Attribution ... allows an author to accumulate a positive reputation that partially repays their work".[4]
