General attributions

These general attributions for OOW Example are of two kinds. The first are the key ideas that inspire and inform OOW Example. The second are content from other sources which spread across Our Own Web (OOW) Example. This content and these ideas from other sources are not just on specific pages but span many or all OOW Example pages.

key ideas:

  1. HTML static browser pages

    Technical, academic, business, government and institutional writing is better with hyper links. Static browser pages are easy to create so write HTML browser pages rather than pages for print on paper. The main exception to writing HTML browser pages I see are creative works like stories where you want to control the reader's path from beginning to end.

  2. wikipedia

    Three ideas taken from wikipedia are

    1. Cite your sources.
    2. Make clear what are internal links and what are external links.
    3. Improve the content. This can be done by a group or by an individual over time.
  3. Free and Open Source Software (FOSS)

    Three ideas taken broadly taken from the FOSS movement are

    1. Think about how you want your work used.
    2. Make the legal statements on your work reflect how you want your work used.

      FOSS and the Creative Commons licenses which extend libre and open licenses into writing and creations other than text are all about getting the legal part regarding your work to do what you want. These licenses also happen to emphasize openness and liberty in the use. Your choices may be a free/libre license or a very proprietary use like a business trade secret or confidential information, or government classified information. Your use may be something in between like the copyright and use typical of books (you can read the book; share the book or resell the book but you cannot copy the book and you especially cannot copy the book and sell the copies). In any case make the legal statements on your work reflect how you want it used.

    3. Use other works according to thier legal use statements.

      If you put care into stating how others are to use your work you might be more likely to follow the use statements of others regarding their works.
