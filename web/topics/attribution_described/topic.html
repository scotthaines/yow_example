Attribution described

Attribution acknowledges the work of another.[1] Good attributions have several parts.[2] These are

  • Indicate what is from another source.
  • title (Provide the title or description of what is from another source.)
  • author (Who created it.)
  • source (Where can you get it.)
  • license (Under what terms is this work provided.)
  • changes (If the work included is modified from the original, a derivative work, then describe the changes.)

YOW Example Attributions are provided for several reasons:

  1. Licenses and terms of use often require attribution. For example the CC BY 4.0 License Deed's license requires the deed's attribution when the deed is included.[3]
  2. It is the right thing to do. "It is widely regarded as a sign of decency and respect to acknowledge the creator by giving him/her credit for the work."[4]
  3. Attributions in YOW Example serve as an example.
  4. "Attribution ... allows an author to accumulate a positive reputation that partially repays their work".[5]
